What is your interest in IT?

I have had a general interest in IT for most of my life. I have built my own computers for the past 15 years and like to keep up to date with all the latest happenings in the world of technology. I like to maintain the latest and greatest gadgets and technology for personal use and enjoy understanding how they work.

When did your interest in IT start? Was there a particular event or person that sparked your interest? Outline your IT experience (if any).

It started when I was a child - my father for a short time wrote for an Amiga magazine and I was always surrounded by computers when I was growing up. I have been working in IT for the past 10 years and currently work in IT for the Department of Premier and Cabinet, South Australia.

Why did you choose to come to RMIT?

It was recommended by an advisor I spoke to at Open Universities Australia. The course material looked interesting, and it looked like there were many different career pathways the degree can take me. This was appealing as before doing the degree, I wasn’t particularly sure what area of IT I wanted to specialise in.

What do you expect to learn during your studies?

I expect to learn specialised skills which will help me when going out into the workforce and to build a career. I expect to learn how IT works in real world contexts which will prepare me for future IT jobs.
